## 2. Overall Description

### 2.1 Executive Summary

ETD Airlines is a lightweight departure-operations web application that gives users a live board of flights, current estimated departure times, operational statuses, disruption context, and summary statistics. Users can search and prioritize flights, create new records, revise ETDs, cancel flights, mark flights departed, and review current KPI and chart outputs. This revised BRD now states explicitly that the **current implementation is a demo/training/reference console with always-on seeded and simulated data**, not a production-ready control environment, because durable records, authorization, audit, and governed external integrations are not implemented. **[Confidence: Confirmed / Inferred — confirmed capabilities from `README.md:1-68`, `FlightController.java:29-69`, `StatisticsService.java:28-85`; always-on simulation confirmed in `README.md:49-55` and `FlightSimulationService.java:35-129`; governance gaps confirmed in `README.md:98-105`, `FlightRepository.java:11-18`, and `WebConfig.java:17-23`]**

### 2.2 Business Background / Current-State Overview

Airport or airline departure operations require frequent review of flight readiness, gate status, delay causes, and departure timing. The application’s interaction model — rapid dashboard refresh, row-level operational actions, and current-shift KPI visibility — strongly indicates a current-state need for operators to manage same-day departures rather than passenger commerce. **[Confidence: Inferred — from `index.html:57-190`, `dashboard.js:24-236`, `statistics.js:8-98`]**

The codebase does not show integrations to authoritative operational systems, identity controls, durable storage, or audit governance, and the application always seeds sample flights and continues simulation every 30 seconds after startup. As a result, the current-state solution should be read as a standalone reference implementation or training console rather than a governed operational source of record. **[Confidence: Confirmed / Inferred — confirmed absence in `pom.xml:28-49`, `FlightRepository.java:11-18`, `README.md:98-105`; always-on simulation confirmed in `FlightSimulationService.java:35-129`]**
