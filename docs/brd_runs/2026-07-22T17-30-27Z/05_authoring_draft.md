## 2. Overall Description

### 2.1 Executive Summary

ETD Airlines is a lightweight departure-operations web application that gives operations staff a live board of flights, current estimated departure times, operational statuses, disruption context, and summary statistics. Users can search and prioritize flights, create new records, revise ETDs, cancel flights, mark flights departed, and review current KPI and chart outputs. The current implementation is suitable as a demo/reference operations console, but its lack of persistence, audit trail, authorization, and real external feeds means it does not yet evidence a production-ready control environment. **[Confidence: Confirmed / Inferred — confirmed capabilities from `README.md:1-68`, `FlightController.java:29-69`, `StatisticsService.java:28-85`; inferred demo/reference posture from `FlightSimulationService.java:35-129`, `README.md:98-105`, open CORS in `WebConfig.java:17-23`]**

### 2.2 Business Background / Current-State Overview

Airport or airline departure operations require frequent review of flight readiness, gate status, delay causes, and departure timing. The application’s interaction model — rapid dashboard refresh, row-level operational actions, and current-shift KPI visibility — strongly indicates a current-state need for operators to manage same-day departures rather than passenger commerce. **[Confidence: Inferred — from `index.html:57-190`, `dashboard.js:24-236`, `statistics.js:8-98`]**

The codebase does not show integrations to authoritative operational systems, identity controls, or durable storage. As a result, the current-state solution appears to function as a standalone reference implementation or training console rather than a governed operational source of record. **[Confidence: Confirmed / Inferred — confirmed absence in `pom.xml:28-49`, `FlightRepository.java:11-18`, `README.md:98-105`; inference explained by seeded/simulated data in `FlightSimulationService.java:35-129`]**
